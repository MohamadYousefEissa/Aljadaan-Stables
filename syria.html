The Syrian Desert is the homeland of Arabian horse lines which were related to names of individuals such as (Jedran, In zobeini, Ibn Aamoud, Ibn Sehian, In Sbeil, Ibn Khidli, Al Sahlan, Shrrak, Sabbah...) These individuals belonged to Arabian tribes that settled in the Syrian desert such as (Shammar, Rualla, Fidaan, Sbaa, Weld Ali, Tai, Al Jbour, Naem, Al Hadadeen, Al Mawali, Bani Khaled....) and we rarely find names of the main lines such as Seglawieh, Obaya, Maanagia, Koheila, Dahma, that are not attached to these names.
A quick look at the history books and stud records reveal that Syria with its ancient cities such as Damascus, Aleppo, Homs, Hama, Deir Ezzour and Palmyra, was the destination for many travellers and searchers who came to obtain Arabian horses. Indeed the majority of the foundation stock of the world breeding programmes were of Syrian origin. After the national campaign of the Syrian Ministry of Agriculture, experts and enthusiasts encouraged by our late Basil Hafez.
Al Assad, started to register Arabian horses in Syria. Furthermore, Syria become a WAHO member. This was an essential step to restore Syria as one of the central countries in the world of the Arabian horses, as well as to assume its role as an important source of the noble breed of the Asil Arabians.

The Arabian horse is known for its beauty, strength, bravery and faithfulness. As such it was most desired by those who wanted to breed it purely, as well as to improve other horse breeds.
There are hardly any horse breeds in the world that do not trace their blood line to the Arabian horses. In this way, the Arabian horse has become a father and founder of many horse breeds in the world.
It deserves to be called the Arabian ambassador to the world. The Arabian horse has occupied high status in the Arabs' life. Islam elevated its position and encouraged its breeding. Moreover it was mentioned in the Holy Quran and in several speeches of Prophet Mohammad.
Ancient and contemporary Arab poets, artists and writers have considered the Arabian horse a rich subject stimulating artistic creation.
